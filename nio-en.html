1. Our Comments

Based on FY2026 Q1 financials and management guidance, the NTAM research team highlights the following core conclusions:

🎯

Core Conclusions

  • 1.
    Strong Multiplied Revenue Growth

    First-quarter total revenue reached RMB 25.53 billion (approx. USD 3.70 billion), a surging year-on-year increase of 112.2%. This was primarily driven by the explosion in new model deliveries and an optimized product mix pushing up the average selling price (ASP).

  • 2.
    Significant Improvement in Margins and Operating Profit

    Benefiting from an increased proportion of high-margin products, vehicle gross margin climbed sharply to 18.8% (up 860 bps YoY); consolidated gross margin also reached 19.0%. Under Non-GAAP metrics, adjusted operating profit turned positive, reaching RMB 66.76 million.

  • 3.
    Multi-Brand Strategy Yielding Initial Success

    Total deliveries this quarter reached 83,465 units, a massive 98.3% increase YoY. Within the multi-brand matrix, the NIO core brand contributed 58,543 units, the new ONVO brand shipped 13,339 units, and the FIREFLY brand delivered 11,583 units, successfully capturing a more diverse segment of the market.

  • 4.
    Organizational Optimization and Robust Cash Cushion

    Due to structural modernization and optimization, R&D and SG&A expenses decreased YoY following personnel allocation adjustments. The company currently holds RMB 48.2 billion (approx. USD 7 billion) in cash reserves, providing a solid cushion for the upcoming mass production of the executive flagship SUV NIO ES9 and ONVO L80.

5. Risk Factors

  1. New Model Production & Supply Chain Scale-up Risks: The new brand ONVO L80 and executive flagship SUV NIO ES9 have just entered intensive shipping phases, facing fierce market share competition and yield rate challenges during core component ramp-ups.
  2. Transition Between Architectures & Sales Volatility: With the pre-sale of multiple new architecture models launched, existing first-generation and legacy models may face promotional price cuts, impacting short-term inventory stability.
  3. Power Replenishment Infrastructure & BaaS Continuous CapEx: Maintaining the leading battery-swap network (BaaS model) requires massive, long-term fixed asset investments. If user subscription conversion fails to meet expectations, it may prolong the break-even cycle.
  4. Macroeconomy & Autonomous Driving Chip Regulatory Dependencies: Iterations of self-developed autonomous driving chips and full-domain vehicle operating systems heavily rely on underlying hardware/software evolution, facing headwinds from global compliance and automotive safety mandates.

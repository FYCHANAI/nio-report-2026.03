I. Our Views

Based on the FY26 Q1 earnings report and management call, below are the core conclusions distilled by the NTAM research team:

🎯

Core Conclusions

  • 1.
    Strong AWS Cloud Growth

    AWS Q1 revenue reached $37.6 billion, up 28% YoY. This is the fastest growth rate in nearly 15 quarters on a massive base, showing that generative AI workloads are tangibly translating into cloud revenue.

  • 2.
    Massive AI CapEx Expansion

    Trailing twelve months free cash flow dropped to $1.2 billion, primarily driven by a $59.3 billion YoY increase in purchases of property and equipment (net of proceeds). This highlights Amazon's massive buildout of AI compute infrastructure.

  • 3.
    Custom AI Chips & Model Ecosystem Boom

    The annual revenue run rate for custom chips (Graviton, Trainium, Nitro) has surpassed $20 billion, growing at triple-digit percentages. Furthermore, OpenAI and Anthropic have committed to leveraging Amazon's Trainium infrastructure to train their advanced AI models.

  • 4.
    Huge Non-Operating Investment Gains

    Q1 net income delivered an impressive $30.3 billion, which includes a $16.8 billion pre-tax gain from the investment in AI startup Anthropic, demonstrating massive returns on its strategic investments.

IV. Wall Street Views

Summarized from the latest Bloomberg ANR investment bank research reports and earnings call commentary:

Morgan Stanley

Overweight
  • AWS Re-acceleration: Highly positive on AWS revenue growth accelerating to 28%. Views this as a clear signal that enterprises are fully shifting from the cloud cost optimization cycle toward AI-driven expansion.
  • Custom Chip Moat: Emphasized that custom chips like Trainium and Inferentia reaching a $20 billion annualized revenue run rate will become a key cost advantage in the long-term AI compute race.

Goldman Sachs

Buy
  • CapEx Expansion Justified: Although a nearly $60 billion YoY increase in CapEx caused a drop in free cash flow, the massive infrastructure commitments from OpenAI and Anthropic provide high certainty for this investment.
  • Retail Margin Expansion: North America retail operating margin reached 7.9% (profit growth of 42%), demonstrating that the benefits of warehouse and delivery regionalization continue to play out, indicating extreme health in the core retail business.

UBS

Buy
  • Investment Gains Validate Vision: The $16.8 billion unrealized gain from Anthropic perfectly validates Amazon's precision in laying out its foundation model ecosystem.
  • Ads & Subs Dual Engines: Highlighted that advertising services (up 24%) and subscription services (up 15%) continue to be crucial engines driving overall gross margin expansion alongside AWS.

V. Risk Factors

  1. Economic & Demand Downturn Risk: The earnings report highlighted that changes in customer demand and spending (including fears of an economic recession), inflation, and interest rate fluctuations could directly impact retail e-commerce and enterprise cloud spending.
  2. Supply Chain & Resource Volatility: Volatility in resources and supply chains; the earnings release specifically called out supply risks for "memory chips," which could limit the expansion speed of AWS datacenters.
  3. Macro & Geopolitics: Global economic conditions, changes in geopolitical circumstances, trade and tariff policies, and the occurrence of unpredictable world events.
  4. FX & Operating Costs: International business is susceptible to foreign exchange rate fluctuations; additionally, energy price volatility and regional labor market constraints could increase cost pressures on logistics and fulfillment.

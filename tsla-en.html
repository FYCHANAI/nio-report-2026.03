I. Our Comments

Based on the latest earnings, delivery data, and management's strategic pivot, the following are the core conclusions extracted by the NTAM research team:

🎯

Key Conclusions

  • 1.
    Deliveries below expectations, production-delivery gap widens

    Q1 total deliveries were 358,023 vehicles, missing the market consensus of ~365k. Total production for the quarter was 408,386, exceeding deliveries by 50,363. This reflects a potential inventory build-up or transit timing issues, though it must be evaluated alongside regional sales and logistics; it should not be equated directly with unsold inventory.

  • 2.
    Strategic focus aggressively shifting to AI, autonomous driving, and infrastructure

    During the earnings call, Elon Musk reiterated that Tesla will increase investments in AI compute, Robotaxi/Cybercab, and related infrastructure. This supports the long-term valuation upside but may push CapEx higher in the near term, pressuring free cash flow. The market needs to see clearer commercialization progress to justify Tesla's valuation pivot from an automaker to an AI/automation platform.

  • 3.
    Energy business remains strategically valuable, but short-term momentum shouldn't be overstated

    Q1 energy storage deployment was 8.8 GWh, remaining a crucial part of Tesla's long-term energy platform. However, compared to Q1 2025's 10.4 GWh, deployment fell by ~15% YoY, and thus should not be described as 'strong growth'. Megapack 3 and capacity expansion can support mid-to-long-term gross margins, but order conversion and deployment pace remain to be watched in the short term.

  • 4.
    FSD regulatory risks remain the primary hurdle for Robotaxi valuation

    NHTSA's probe into Tesla's FSD / camera perception systems reflects ongoing market concerns regarding the safety of the vision-only approach in glaring light, dust, and low-visibility environments. Tesla's software updates may mitigate some risks, but actual effectiveness awaits regulatory assessment. For investors, realizing the Robotaxi valuation depends not just on technical progress, but critically on regulatory approvals, liability assignment, and a timeline for mass commercialization.

V. Risk Factors

  1. Production-Delivery Gap, Potential Inventory & Margin Pressure: Q1 2026 production exceeded deliveries by 50,363 vehicles, reflecting inventory build-up or transit delays. If price cuts or promotions are needed to digest this inventory, auto gross margins may be further squeezed.
  2. Surging CapEx, Near-Term FCF Under Pressure: Tesla is ramping up investments in AI compute, Robotaxi/Cybercab, Energy, and related infrastructure. This supports long-term growth options but will push CapEx higher in the near term, potentially limiting FCF support for its valuation.
  3. Unresolved FSD Regulatory & Litigation Risks: NHTSA has opened an in-depth probe into Tesla FSD's system detection and driver alert capabilities in low-visibility environments. If the outcome mandates recalls, additional software fixes, or stricter disclosures, Tesla could face rising compliance, legal, and brand trust costs.
  4. Intensifying EV Price Competition & Pressure from Chinese OEMs: Global EV demand is not weak across the board, but growth pace diverges by region. Tesla's primary risk is intensifying price competition in China and Europe, coupled with pressure from Chinese OEMs like BYD regarding costs, product refreshes, and overseas expansion, which could limit Tesla's market share and valuation multiples.
